How it works

Three mechanics that make your graph alive.

Most knowledge tools are passive. NANTA does work between your sessions — quietly, sparsely, and with the entire graph as context.

01

Evolving thread on every article

The moment NANTA generates an article, newsletter, or podcast from your sources, it opens a research thread for the topic.

Every 24 hours, the thread polls the web for related new sources — articles, news, video — and links them back to the original. Your generated piece becomes the spine of an evolving story you can scroll through over months.

  • Set a custom focus per thread to steer the queries
  • Choose cadence: 4h / 24h / 7d
  • Pause, resume, or stop tracking anytime

02

Spider research across the graph

Every six hours, NANTA scores every entity in your graph by recency, connectivity, and total mass — then picks the single hottest topic that hasn't been researched in three days.

It generates queries that combine that topic with its graph neighbors — entities that already co-occur in your sources. Each poll surfaces stories that don't just match a keyword; they bridge what you already know.

  • Picks one topic per cycle — sparse by design
  • Bridge queries grow your graph laterally, not just deeper
  • 72-hour per-topic cooldown — never spammy

03

Daily podcast, narrated locally

Once every 24 hours, NANTA picks the most active recent topic in your graph and writes a 10–15 minute briefing on it — journalistic tone, real synthesis across your sources.

Then it speaks the script using Kokoro, a free local neural voice — no ElevenLabs subscription, no audio leaving your machine. Wake up to a custom briefing on whatever you've been reading most.

  • British male news-anchor voice by default — change it via PODCAST_VOICE
  • Requires ≥3 fresh sources on the topic — sparse, never repetitive
  • 168-hour per-topic cooldown
